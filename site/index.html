Shift handoff memory

The morning shift should not start from zero.

The app gives a team one shared case history for what changed, what still needs attention, and why it matters. A worker leaves a note. The next shift gets a cited handoff. A supervisor fixes mistakes. The team stops rebuilding context from scratch.

Why this exists

Handoffs fail when the next person has to reconstruct the past.

Most tools only work after someone gathers all the context. Real shift work is the opposite. The context is already scattered across notes, files, small decisions, and things people forgot to repeat.

This is not trying to be a medical assistant. It is a handoff system: keep useful facts, cite where they came from, let a human correct them, and remove what should not stay.

The product flow

A simple workflow for the team. A reliable case history underneath.

  1. 1

    Leave the shift note

    The worker writes what changed. To them, it feels like normal note-taking.

    Saved to the case history.
  2. 2

    Generate the next handoff

    The next shift asks for the handoff and sees a short, source-backed brief.

    Relevant prior notes are pulled in.
  3. 3

    Correct what matters

    A supervisor marks a detail important or fixes the way it was summarized.

    Future handoffs prioritize the correction.
  4. 4

    Remove what should not stay

    Incorrect, stale, or sensitive details can be removed from future handoffs.

    Removed details stop appearing.
How it works

The product feels like a handoff board, not a technical console.

The UI stays human: notes, handoffs, questions, corrections, and deletion. Behind the scenes, the backend keeps a case-scoped history service and only passes relevant, authorized context into generated answers.

For reviewers

The submission separates product experience from engineering proof.

Normal users should know whether the handoff is useful and where each claim came from. They should not have to understand the technical storage layer.

Judges and engineers get the proof: Gemini recall planning, Cognee-backed case memory, backend-only API keys, source references, operation traces, deletion behavior, and SRE runbooks.
